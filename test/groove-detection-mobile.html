Step 1 · Start the audio engine

Asks for mic access, starts the timing engine, and loads the onset detector. Grant the mic prompt when it appears.
Use headphones. If the click plays through speakers, the mic hears it and logs phantom hits. Headphones keep the click out of the signal.

Step 2 · Tune the detector to your bass & room

Detect only: play notes and watch the energy meter fire. No timing — this is purely to confirm it catches every note with no doubles or phantom hits. Tune Sensitivity / Min gap until the hit count matches what you played.

How to read the result

Play steady quarter notes straight to the click. The thing that decides viability is Consistency (std dev), because it's unaffected by latency — it measures only how noisy the detection timestamps are.

Std dev under ~15 ms → detection is tight enough to build a real trainer on. 15–30 ms → usable but borderline; you'd lean on coarser feedback. Over ~30 ms → the mic path is too noisy on bass, and the differentiating feature won't hold up (this is the answer you need to know now, not after building it).

Also watch Hits vs expected in Detect-only: if it misses notes or fires twice on one, fix that with Sensitivity / Min gap before trusting any timing number. Try it on different basses, pickup positions, and tones — a clean bridge-pickup attack detects far better than a dark neck-pickup thump.
