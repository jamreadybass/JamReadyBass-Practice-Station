How to use PocketLocker

1 · Start audio. On the Play tab, tap Enable mic & click and allow the microphone. Headphones (or an interface with headphone monitoring) are best. PocketLocker checks your room automatically.

2 · Set your instrument. Open Setup → Detection test and tap 4-string or 5-string. That one tap tunes the mic so every note counts once — no phantom hits from ringing strings. (See Dial it in below to fine-tune.)

3 · Calibrate. Hit Play; when it asks, tap Calibrate now and play ~16 steady notes locked to the click. This measures your device's audio delay so your notes land in the right slot. Re-calibrate if you switch headphones or interface.

4 · Pick a mode. Challenge, Freeplay, or Random — tap one to open its setup box. The amber bar up top always shows your current mode and settings.

5 · Play the Challenge. Tap Challenge, choose a difficulty (Easy → Medium → Hard → Pro) and any level you've unlocked. Read the staff up top and play each note the instant its gem crosses the amber pocket line. Clear a level to unlock the next; clear all 12 to move up a difficulty.

6 · Read your scores. Center = on your chosen feel. Evenness = consistency. Rhythm = right notes. Stars to advance: Easy 1, Medium 2, Hard and Pro 3. Pro also hides the gems — you read the notation.

Controls. Spacebar pauses and resumes a run; Stop ends it.

Dial it in — detection & calibration

Get detection accurate. Open Setup → Detection test and tap Start test. Tap your instrument preset, then play 8 steady notes on each string — ringing and muted. Watch the count: each note should register once. Muted should read 8; ringing should land near 8 too.

If ringing over-counts (phantom hits from sustain): open Advanced and raise Trigger threshold a little, then Ring rejection. If a low B or low E over-triggers, raise Low-cut. Nudge until ringing reads 8 while muted still reads 8.

If soft notes get missed: lower Trigger threshold, or lower Low-cut so it hears the low strings. There's always a trade-off between catching soft notes and rejecting ring — the knobs let you pick the balance. The 5-string preset (Trigger 1.70 · Low-cut 195 · Ring 0.16) is a solid starting point.

Calibrate your timing. Every device delays the sound a little; without calibrating, every note reads late. Play → Calibrate now → play ~16 steady quarter notes to the click. Click mode and Visual-only (muted) mode keep separate offsets — calibrate each once.

Fine-tune from a real run. If your notes read a touch early or late overall, play a run and tap Center from last run — it learns your offset from how you actually played. Re-calibrate whenever you change headphones or interface.

Troubleshooting

Extra / phantom notes. Tap your instrument preset first. Still over-counting? In Detection test → Advanced, raise Trigger threshold and Ring rejection. Keep Speaker mode on; headphones help most.

Notes aren't registering. In Detection test → Advanced, lower Trigger threshold, or lower Low-cut so it hears your low strings — or just dig in a little harder.

Notes read early or late. Calibrate, or tap Center from last run after a run to auto-correct.

Gems don't line up (Bluetooth lag). Adjust Highway sync until a gem meets the click. Never affects your score.

Phone, no headphones. Turn on Visual-only beat — the click goes silent and the pocket pulses; play to the pulse.

A level's locked. Levels unlock one at a time per difficulty — clear the current one to open the next. Switch difficulty in the Challenge box.

Start over. Reset challenge progress is at the top of the Challenge box (tap twice to confirm). It keeps your calibration and detection tuning.
